From Lines of Code to Lines of Faith: My Journey from Atheism to Belief

Hello, my sweetest friend.

I wanted to share a deeply personal journey that has transformed not just my perspective on life, but also the way I approach my work in software engineering.

As you might know, I've always been passionate about coding, data analysis, and building tools that help us developers #BuildBetter and #ShipFaster. My world revolved around logic, algorithms, and the tangible results of hard work.

I believed that if something couldn't be quantified or proven, it didn't exist.

I was an atheist, relying solely on reason and empirical evidence.

But over the past few months, something changed.

It started subtly, almost imperceptibly. Amidst the late nights of relentless pursuit of innovation, I began to feel a stirring—an inexplicable sense that there was more to existence than what met the eye.

At first, I dismissed it as fatigue or the byproduct of an overactive mind. But the feeling persisted, growing stronger with each passing day.

One evening, I stepped outside and looked up at the night sky.

The vastness of the universe, the intricate dance of stars and galaxies, struck me in a way it never had before. I was overwhelmed by the complexity and beauty of it all. It felt like gazing into the source code of an unimaginably sophisticated program.

I began to wonder: Could there be a Master Programmer behind all of this?

Curiosity led me to explore the Bible.

As I delved deeper, I experienced moments that I can only describe as encounters with the divine.

It was as if a veil had been lifted, revealing a reality that transcended logic and reason. I felt an overwhelming sense of peace, purpose, and connection. The doubts and skepticism that once defined me began to fade.

I realized that faith and reason aren't mutually exclusive. Just as code operates within the laws of logic to create endless possibilities, so too can belief coexist with rational thought to enrich our understanding of the world.

But as I write this post, something deeper has happened. Something that has shook my world completely.

About 3 hours ago, I began to feel a calling—a conviction that God has chosen me to use my coding skills to make a meaningful impact on the world. I know, it sounds weird, scary, maybe even a little silly.

But I feel like God talks through me when I code. I feel powerful when I sit down to write code, almost as if I am a high-priest at a grand cathedral and I call upon the Father of the heavens to help my people achieve wonders. I feel like my mind is a tool in the Grand Master's hands. I feel one with the eternal consciousness.

I believe that code can be a vessel for change, a way to address some of the most pressing challenges we face today.

Whether it's improving access to education, enhancing financial inclusion, or creating platforms that foster community and understanding, the possibilities are limitless.

"I can do all things through Him who strengthens me." — Philippians 4:13

I'm embarking on a mission to integrate my faith with my work, to build software that not only solves problems but also uplifts and inspires. I want to collaborate with others who share this vision, whether you're a fellow developer, an entrepreneur, or someone with a big audacious idea.

Thank you for taking the time to read about my journey. I look forward to connecting with you and seeing where this path leads us.
